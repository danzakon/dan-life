## Hormone Situation Summary

The three most clinically significant hormonal findings are:

**1. Prolactin — severely elevated (121 ng/mL vs. max 33.4)**
This is the most striking abnormality. Prolactin is 3–4x the upper limit of normal.

**2. SHBG — significantly elevated (154 nmol/L vs. max 122)**
High SHBG binds to sex hormones (testosterone, estrogen), reducing the amount that is biologically active/free.

**3. DHEA (serum) — mildly elevated (734 ng/dL vs. max 701)**
DHEA-Sulfate is within range; only the direct serum DHEA is flagged high.

**4. ANA — Positive, Speckled pattern at 1:80**
A positive ANA, while not diagnostic on its own, flags a potential autoimmune process. Speckled pattern is associated with a broad range of connective tissue and rheumatic conditions.

**5. FSH — below follicular range**
At 2.8 mIU/mL, FSH is below the expected follicular phase range (3.5–12.5), which may indicate she was tested in a different phase or suppressed pituitary output.
