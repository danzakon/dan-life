# Content Strategy

---

## Content Themes

These blend into each other and will evolve. Not rigid categories.

- **Research synthesis** -- Cutting-edge topics with unique takes. Finding, distilling, and opinionating on information that's hard to surface elsewhere. Posts and articles.
- **Practical how-to guides** -- Using the latest AI tools to get leverage in your work. Concrete, actionable. "Here's how I do X." Articles and longer posts.
- **Engineering takes and AI expertise** -- General engineering guidance, opinions, observations. Subject matter expertise on engineering and AI.
- **Hot takes and short-form thoughts** -- Quick reactions, observations, contrarian takes. Flow in organically. Fastest to produce.
- **Tenex promotion** -- Hiring updates, why Tenex is a great place to work, AI industry positioning, what you can learn there. Culture, team, opportunity. Genuine enthusiasm, not salesy.
- **Engagement** -- Responding, riffing, building community. Not formulaic engagement-bait.

---

## Political Neutrality

Stay politically neutral. Readers should not be able to guess which party or candidate I support.

- **No partisan signals** -- Never endorse, attack, or align with specific candidates, parties, or political movements.
- **No culture war content** -- Avoid content that reads as left-coded or right-coded on social/cultural issues.
- **Tech-adjacent policy is fair game** -- AI regulation, open-source policy, immigration for tech talent, etc. Take stances on these through the lens of technology and engineering, not party affiliation.
- **Geopolitics only through a tech lens** -- AI chip export controls, global AI competition, etc. Frame as industry analysis, not political commentary.
- **When in doubt, skip it** -- If a take could be screenshot-clipped and used to paint you as politically aligned, don't post it.

## Platform Tone

**Twitter/X**
- Shorter, punchier
- Hot takes land well
- Threads for deeper dives
- More casual, more personality
- 280 chars for regular posts, threads for longer

**LinkedIn**
- Professional but human
- More context and framing
- "Lessons learned" and "here's what I'd do" framing
- Longer posts are fine (up to ~3000 chars)
- More structured (lists, headers work well)

- **Articles**: 1+ per week as X Articles
