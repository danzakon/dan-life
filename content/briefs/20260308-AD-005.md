## Core Insight
Tech companies are becoming power utilities by necessity, not choice, and the implications for American energy infrastructure are massive.

## Draft Instructions
The BYOP (Bring Your Own Power) movement: OpenAI, xAI, Meta, Google building on-site power plants because grid interconnection queues now stretch 4-8 years. This is the "energy Wild West" framing from the research. The argument: something fundamental is breaking about the utility model that has defined American energy for a century. Tech companies aren't doing this because they want to — they're doing it because the alternative is waiting a decade to train their next model. What does it mean when the world's largest tech companies become energy producers? The regulatory implications, the grid implications, the concentration implications. This is a bigger structural shift than it appears.
