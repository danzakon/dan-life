## Core Insight
AI has made individual scientists dramatically more productive while quietly narrowing the topics science actually explores.

## Draft Instructions
Lead with the paradox stat: AI-augmented scientists publish 3.02x more papers and receive 4.84x more citations — but AI-driven research covers 4.6% less topic territory than conventional studies. The framing is "AI supercharged the scientists and shrunk the science." The deeper argument is about intellectual monoculture: LLMs trained on popular papers push researchers toward already-crowded, data-rich problems. This is the failure mode nobody is talking about. Make the case that quantity is accelerating and diversity is dying, and that's a problem for science long-term.
