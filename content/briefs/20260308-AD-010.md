## Core Insight
Managed voice platforms work well for prototypes, but roughly half the teams building serious products on them outgrow them within a year and face a painful rebuild.

## Draft Instructions
The argument: ElevenLabs, Vapi, Retell — all hit predictable walls. Complex dialogue, cross-call memory, multi-agent handoffs, custom reasoning loops, cost at scale. The visual workflow builders turn into "spaghetti" as complexity grows. The data point: "roughly half the teams we talk to migrate off [managed platforms] within 12 months once they hit scale or customization limits." At >50K minutes/month, custom build (LiveKit/Pipecat) saves up to 80% vs managed platforms. The key tradeoff table: managed = 3 hours to first call, $500-3100/month for 10K mins; custom = 2+ weeks, ~$800 + engineering time. Make the case that there's a right time to build your own stack and how to know when you've hit it. Don't make this about healthcare specifically — frame it around any complex conversational AI product.
