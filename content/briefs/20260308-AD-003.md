## Core Insight
The physical bottleneck for the AI revolution isn't GPUs or research talent — it's skilled tradespeople, and nobody is building the pipeline for them at scale.

## Draft Instructions
Lead with Jensen Huang at Davos 2026: "a lot of six-figure jobs in plumbing and construction." Then build the case: 439,000-worker construction shortage, electricians and MEP specialists the critical path to every new data center, data center construction paying 30% more than typical construction. The argument: discourse is focused on AI researchers and model capabilities while the actual constraint is physical infrastructure workers. You cannot train AI in buildings that don't exist, and the buildings are behind because there aren't enough electricians. Community colleges and trade programs have an urgent opportunity here that policy people aren't addressing. Take a strong stance — this is the most undercovered story in the AI infrastructure buildout.
