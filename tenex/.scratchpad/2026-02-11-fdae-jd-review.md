# Forward Deployed AI Engineer JD Review

**Type:** Hiring
**Status:** [ ] In Progress

---

## Overall

The bones are there but it reads a bit generic. A lot of this could describe any senior eng role at a consulting shop. The thing that makes this role special (frontier AI, building full platforms, agent orchestration, uncapped comp tied to output) gets diluted by safe, broad language. The titles especially need work. Several sound like LinkedIn buzzwords rather than something that would make a top engineer stop scrolling.

---

## The Role Section

> "The Forward AI Engineer at Tenex is an outcome-driven builder working in a high-talent, AI-native environment."

- "outcome-driven builder" and "high-talent, AI-native environment" are empty calories. Every company says this.
- "Forward AI Engineer" drops "Deployed" from the title. Typo or intentional?

> "You partner closely with strategists and clients to design and ship AI-powered workflows that deliver real, measurable impact."

- "AI-powered workflows" undersells it. Based on our earlier conversation this is about building full platforms that transform how businesses operate, not just workflows.

> "Success is defined by what you produce."

- This line is actually great. Keep it.

> "leverage cutting-edge AI tools and internal automations"

- "leverage" and "cutting-edge" are overused. Consider just saying what you mean plainly.

**Suggested rewrite for The Role section:**
> The Forward Deployed AI Engineer at Tenex works directly with clients and strategists to design and build AI-powered platforms that transform how businesses operate. You'll scope solutions, architect systems, and ship production code across multiple engagements simultaneously.
>
> Success is defined by what you produce. You'll have high autonomy, use the best AI tools available to multiply your output, and earn uncapped compensation tied directly to what you deliver.

## What You'll Do: Title-by-Title

### "Forward Deployed Engineering"
- The title is just the job title restated. Doesn't tell you anything new.
- "embedding directly into client codebases and sprint cycles" is good and specific.
- "Ship production-quality code that moves the needle on real business outcomes" is generic. Every eng JD says this.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> **Client-Embedded Delivery** – Own end-to-end delivery across Tenex's client engagements, embedding directly into client codebases and sprint cycles. You ship code that solves real business problems, not hypothetical ones.

### "AI-Augmented Development"
- Title is fine but forgettable.
- "operate at a velocity that redefines what a single engineer can deliver" is doing a lot of heavy lifting. It's a big claim with no specifics. What does this actually look like day-to-day?
- This bullet doesn't mention that you're expected to constantly find and adopt new tools/workflows yourself. It makes it sound like the tooling is handed to you.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> **Build with Maximum Leverage** – Use the latest agentic coding tools, workflow techniques, and internal Tenex tooling to multiply your output. You're expected to constantly find and adopt better ways of working, not wait for them to be handed to you.

### "Full-Stack Development"
- This is the most generic title possible. Every startup puts "full-stack" in their JD. It doesn't differentiate.
- "Ramp into unfamiliar codebases fast and deliver like you've been there from day one" is actually a strong line. The multi-client context-switching reality comes through here.
- But the title buries it. The real point is about versatility and speed across diverse client problems, not "full-stack."

**Suggested rewrite:**
> **Versatile by Default** – Drive execution across diverse tech stacks and problem domains. You'll jump between client engagements, ramp into unfamiliar codebases quickly, and deliver like you've been there from day one.

### "AI-Powered Solutions"
- Title overlaps with "AI-Augmented Development." Two of six bullets start with "AI-[something]" and they blur together.
- "automated workflows, agents, and AI-native product experiences" is closer to the real work but still reads like a feature list. Missing the architecture/systems design angle. You're not just building features, you're designing agent systems and orchestrating complex AI platforms.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> **Systems Design & Agent Architecture** – Partner with AI Strategists to design and build full platforms that transform how businesses operate. You're an architect and an agent orchestrator, building reliable, auditable systems with clean UX that keeps humans in the loop.

### "Sprint Execution & Delivery"
- This title sounds like project management, not engineering. "Sprint Execution" is not what gets a top engineer excited.
- The content is fine (own breakdown, estimation, delivery) but it reads like table stakes, not a differentiator. Every agile shop does sprints.
- The real story here is: you run multiple client workstreams in parallel, you ship at high volume, and your output directly determines your comp. That's way more compelling than "sprint execution."

**Suggested rewrite:**
> **High-Output, Multi-Client Delivery** – Run concurrent workstreams across multiple clients, managing your own priorities and context-switching effectively. Your output is measured in story points and client outcomes, and your comp is uncapped. The more you ship, the more you earn.

### "Continuous Learning"
- Generic title. Could be on any JD at any company.
- The content is actually good ("share what you learn, new AI workflows, reusable patterns, hard-won debugging insights") but the title doesn't earn your attention.
- Missing the real weight here: the tech changes weekly, our clients pay us to be experts on what's next, so it's on you to stay ahead independently. That's different from "continuous learning" which sounds like optional lunch-and-learns.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> **Stay on the Frontier** – The AI landscape changes weekly and our clients pay us to know what's next. Own your learning. Stay ahead of new models, tooling, and techniques, and bring what you find back to the team so everyone gets sharper together.

## Biggest Gaps

1. **No mention of designing systems / architecture.** The role is about being an architect and agent orchestrator, not just shipping features. This is a major differentiator that's completely absent.
2. **The multi-client intensity is buried.** Context switching across multiple engagements, parallelizing work, managing your own priorities. This is core to the role and barely comes through.
3. **The frontier/learning angle is weak.** "Continuous Learning" as the last bullet undersells it. The reality is: the tech evolves weekly, clients pay for expertise in what's new, and it's on you to independently become an expert as things change. That's a requirement, not a nice-to-have.
4. **Uncapped comp story is missing from "What You'll Do."** It's in the comp section but it should be woven into the responsibilities. The fact that output = earnings is a huge differentiator for the right candidate.
5. **"Workflows" appears too much.** The role is about building platforms and systems that transform businesses, not just automating workflows.
