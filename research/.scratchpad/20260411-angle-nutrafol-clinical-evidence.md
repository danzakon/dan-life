# Nutrafol: Clinical Evidence Quality

**Category:** Health Research
**Date Started:** 2026-04-11
**Status:** [x] Complete

---

## Summary
Nutrafol's clinical evidence is entirely company-funded with significant methodological limitations. No independent replications exist. A registered trial with stricter criteria was never published.

## Key Findings

### Study Design Issues
- Pivotal 2018 women's trial: only 40 participants (26 active vs 14 placebo), unequal randomization
- Placebo group was older with higher baseline stress — stacking the deck
- Excluded women with actual androgenic alopecia, thyroid disorders, medications affecting hair
- Participants had "self-perceived thinning" not clinically diagnosed hair loss
- Effect was modest: ~10.4% terminal hair increase at 180 days vs 3.5% placebo
- 2025 men's trial: primary endpoint (terminal hair counts via HairMetrix) not analyzable due to protocol deviations

### The Buried Sadick Trial
- Nutrafol registered TWO trials on ClinicalTrials.gov (NCT03206567 published, NCT03052413 never published)
- Sadick trial had stricter criteria: clinically confirmed female pattern hair loss
- Results never published — "almost always due to unfavorable findings"

### Funding Bias
- Every published Nutrafol study funded by Nutraceutical Wellness Inc.
- Dr. Sophia Kogan (Nutrafol co-founder) co-authored all studies
- Lead researchers are paid advisors/consultants
- Corresponding author on 2025 men's trial: isabelle@nutrafol.com (employee)

### Independent Reviews
- 2026 Frontiers NMA ranked Nutrafol highest among supplements (SUCRA 80.8%) but used the same company-funded data
- GRADE assessment: "low certainty evidence" for terminal hair density
- No truly independent replication exists
- Bayesian NMA: Nutrafol growth effect plateaus after 12 weeks

### Skeptical Dermatologists
- Dr. Robert Bernstein: "no scientific evidence that Nutrafol can actually reverse or mitigate androgenetic alopecia"
- DermMythBuster: effective for "modest ~10% increase in otherwise healthy women who would like thicker hair" but "no data for patients with female pattern hair loss"
- Dr. Brian Yeung: "studies mostly suggest it might help with self-perceived thinning, particularly in Caucasian women"

### Legal Issues
- TINA.org filed FTC/FDA complaint (April 2023) over "clinically proven" claims
- Class action lawsuits filed (Smith v. Nutraceutical, Sheehan v. Nutraceutical)
- Proprietary Synergen Complex prevents independent verification
