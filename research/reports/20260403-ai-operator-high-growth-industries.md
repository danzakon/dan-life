# High-Growth Industries for Solo AI-Powered Operators

**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Context:** Identifying markets with massive proven demand where a solo AI-powered operator could capture significant value -- modeled on how Medvi ($1.8B, one person, $20K starting capital) rode the GLP-1 wave.

---

## The Medvi Blueprint

Before evaluating opportunities, it is worth distilling what made Medvi work:

1. **Massive existing demand** -- GLP-1 drugs already had millions of people actively searching for them
2. **Regulatory bottleneck** -- needed a doctor's prescription, creating a chokepoint that could be automated (telehealth)
3. **High ticket, recurring revenue** -- $300-500/month per patient, ongoing
4. **AI as the operations layer** -- customer service, intake forms, scheduling, follow-up all automated
5. **Licensed professionals as a service** -- doctors contracted, not employed
6. **Zero investors** -- bootstrapped with $20K, scaled on revenue

The ideal next opportunity shares these traits: proven demand, regulatory or complexity bottleneck, high-ticket or recurring revenue, and work that is mostly coordination and paperwork (not physical labor).

## 1. Climate/Energy Transition

### Market Size & Growth
- U.S. residential solar market: ~$30B+ annually
- U.S. residential heat pump market: growing rapidly, projected to double by 2035
- EV charging installation: $5B+ market, 30%+ CAGR
- Total residential energy transition: $100B+ opportunity over next decade
- IRA provides 30% federal tax credit for solar, heat pumps, EV chargers (though OBBBA may reduce some credits)

### Demand Signal
**VERY STRONG.** EnergySage reports a historic shift in homeowner behavior as tax credit deadlines approach. Heat pump rebates ($8K HEAR grants) are rolling out state by state. Homeowner demand is real and surging -- people actively searching for these services.

### Infrastructure to Build On
- EnergySage (solar marketplace), HeatPumpNetwork.com (rebate aggregator)
- State-level contractor licensing databases
- IRA rebate programs with state-by-state rollout (HOMES/HEAR programs)
- Utility company rebate programs

### What the Solo Operator Would Do vs. Outsource
| Solo operator (AI) | Outsource |
|---------------------|-----------|
| Lead generation, intake forms | Actual installation (licensed contractors) |
| Subsidy/rebate calculation engine | Electrical/permitting work |
| Contractor matching & scheduling | Engineering assessments |
| Customer follow-up & project tracking | Utility interconnection |
| Document preparation for permits | |

### Revenue Model
- **Lead generation fees:** $200-500 per qualified lead to contractors
- **Project management fee:** 5-10% of project cost ($1,500-3,000 per install)
- **Rebate navigation fee:** Flat fee ($200-500) for handling paperwork
- **Margins:** 40-60% (no physical inventory, no labor)

### Why This Hasn't Been Done (or Has It?)
EnergySage exists as a marketplace but is a VC-backed platform, not an AI-first operator. The gap is in the **concierge layer** -- navigating the confusing maze of federal, state, utility, and local incentives for homeowners. Most homeowners leave money on the table because the rebate landscape is too complex.

### Key Risks
- **Political risk:** IRA credits could be reduced further under current administration (OBBBA already trimming some)
- **Contractor dependency:** Quality of work is outside your control
- **Licensing requirements:** Some states require contractor licenses even for project management
- **Seasonality:** Demand is cyclical

### Verdict: ★★★☆☆
Strong demand but heavy dependency on physical contractors and political risk around subsidies. The "rebate navigation" niche is most viable as a solo AI play, but it is a smaller slice of the pie.

## 2. Immigration Services

### Market Size & Growth
- U.S. immigration legal services: **$8.7B** (2023), growing 3.4% annually
- Projected to reach **$7.5B** (just legal fees, excluding gov fees) by 2028 at 7.9% CAGR
- USCIS received **9.8 million** immigration benefit requests in FY2023
- Court backlog: **3.2 million** pending cases
- 3.5 million pending naturalization applications
- 1.4 million H-1B petitions filed for FY2024 cap alone

### Demand Signal
**OVERWHELMINGLY STRONG.** This is arguably the single strongest demand signal on this list:
- Average wait for a Master Calendar hearing: **900 days**
- Only **37%** of immigrants in removal proceedings have legal counsel
- Only **14%** of detained immigrants have representation
- Represented immigrants are **5x more likely** to win their cases
- Average H-1B visa wait time from India: **450 days**
- 80% of corporate immigration filings already handled through digital platforms

### Infrastructure to Build On
- LegalOS (YC W26) -- AI-native immigration law firm delivering visa petitions in 48 hours
- Imagility -- AI-powered immigration software for attorneys
- Gale -- $2.7M funded, AI visa application automation
- Existing attorney networks willing to provide oversight (flat fee per review)
- USCIS e-filing systems
- 98% of immigration forms available for electronic preparation

### What the Solo Operator Would Do vs. Outsource
| Solo operator (AI) | Outsource |
|---------------------|-----------|
| Client intake and eligibility screening | Attorney review and signature (required by law) |
| Form filling and document preparation | Complex litigation/court appearances |
| Case status tracking and updates | Specialized legal strategy |
| Document collection and organization | |
| Deadline management and reminders | |
| WhatsApp/multilingual client communication | |
| RFE (Request for Evidence) response drafting | |

### Revenue Model
- **H-1B petition preparation:** $2,000-3,500 (vs. $5,200 average attorney fee)
- **Family green card package:** $1,500-2,500 (vs. $3,800 median attorney fee)
- **Naturalization application:** $500-800 (vs. $1,200 average attorney fee)
- **DACA renewal:** $300-500 (vs. $800 average attorney fee)
- **Monthly retainer (corporate):** $2,000-5,000/month for ongoing immigration management
- **Margins:** 60-75% (attorney review is the main cost, ~$200-500 per case flat fee)

### Why This Hasn't Been Done (or Has It?)
It is being done -- LegalOS just came out of YC W26 doing exactly this. But the market is so vast (9.8M requests/year, 38,707 existing firms, 80% of which have <5 attorneys) that there is room for many players. The key insight: **42% of clients find lawyers through social media (Facebook/TikTok)**. A solo operator targeting a specific community (e.g., Indian H-1B applicants, which are 72% of all H-1B demand) with native-language content could build a massive funnel.

### Key Risks
- **Unauthorized practice of law (UPL):** Must have attorney oversight. Cannot give legal advice without a licensed attorney involved.
- **Regulatory changes:** Immigration policy shifts constantly (current administration is particularly volatile)
- **Malpractice liability:** Errors can destroy people's lives and immigration status
- **Client trust:** Immigration clients are often vulnerable and need high-touch reassurance

### Verdict: ★★★★★
**This is the closest analog to Medvi.** Massive proven demand, overwhelmed existing providers, high-emotion clients willing to pay, work that is 80%+ form-filling and document preparation (AI's sweet spot), and a clear model for attorney oversight (like Medvi's doctor oversight). The 42% social media acquisition channel means a solo operator with good content can build a massive funnel cheaply.

**The timing could not be better.** Millions of borrowers will need help in Q3 2026. The work is pure data analysis and form-filling -- perfect for AI. The risk is that this is a cyclical opportunity (demand spikes then normalizes) rather than a durable business. Best paired with ongoing refinancing affiliate revenue for long-term income.

- **Crowded market:** Many established players with brand recognition
- **Race to the bottom on price:** Formation is becoming commoditized ($0 + state fees offered by some providers)
- **UPL concerns:** Must be careful not to cross into legal advice
- **Low switching costs:** Customers form once and may not need ongoing services

### Verdict: ★★★★★
**Extremely strong fit.** Proven demand at massive scale, work is 95% form-filling and document generation, recurring revenue through compliance/registered agent, and the market is large enough to support many players. The key is to pick a niche (e.g., "LLC formation for content creators" or "multi-state compliance for e-commerce") and own that audience. Unlike the incumbents, a solo AI operator has near-zero overhead.

## 10. Government Contracting

### Revenue Model
- **SAM.gov registration package:** $500-1,500 (vs. USFCR's $2,500-4,000)

- **Annual compliance maintenance:** $500-1,000/year

- **Long sales cycles:** Government contracts take months to award
- **Client sophistication varies wildly:** Some clients are savvy, many are completely clueless
- **Tool dependency:** Relying on SAM.gov and other government systems that are slow and buggy

### Verdict: ★★★★☆
**Strong opportunity.** The demand is proven ($833B in contracts, 23% set aside for small businesses), the existing players charge premium prices, and the work is heavily document-centric (AI's strength). The best entry point is SAM registration + capability statement + opportunity matching as a bundled service. Recurring revenue through monthly opportunity alerts.

## Top 3 Recommendations

### #1: Immigration Services (The "Next Medvi")

**Why it wins:**
- Closest structural analog to Medvi: massive demand, regulatory bottleneck (attorney requirement), high emotion, largely paperwork-driven
- 9.8 million annual USCIS requests, 3.2 million case backlog, only 37% representation rate
- Clear path to attorney oversight model (like Medvi's doctor oversight)
- 42% of clients come through social media -- cheap acquisition for a content-savvy operator
- Can niche down by visa type (H-1B) or community (Indian, Chinese, Latin American)
- Revenue per customer: $1,500-5,000

**Suggested approach:** Target H-1B applicants (1.4M petitions/year, 72% Indian nationals). Build a TikTok/YouTube funnel in English and Hindi. AI handles intake, eligibility screening, form preparation, document collection, and case tracking. Contract with 2-3 immigration attorneys for oversight at flat rate per case ($200-500). Price at 40-50% below traditional attorney fees.

### #2: Small Business Formation & Compliance (The Cash Machine)

**Why it wins:**
- 5.5 million new businesses per year -- the market literally creates itself
- Work is 95% form-filling and document generation
- Strong recurring revenue (registered agent + annual compliance)
- Can start with near-zero overhead and scale on revenue
- Niche targeting avoids direct competition with LegalZoom

**Suggested approach:** Pick a niche audience (content creators, e-commerce sellers, real estate investors). Build a conversational AI that walks them through entity selection, files the LLC, gets the EIN, generates operating agreement, and sets up registered agent service. Charge $299 for formation + $149/year for compliance monitoring. At 5,500 customers/year (0.1% of new formations), that is $1.6M in formation revenue + growing recurring base.

### #3: Student Loan Optimization (The Time Bomb)

**Why it wins:**
- SAVE plan just died. Millions of borrowers need help RIGHT NOW.
- 90-day window starting July 2026 creates extreme urgency
- The work is pure data analysis -- loan balances, income, repayment plan comparison
- Existing human consultants charge $500+ and are capacity-constrained
- Massive affiliate revenue opportunity through refinancing partners

**Suggested approach:** Launch before July 2026. Build an AI tool that analyzes a borrower's complete loan portfolio, compares all repayment options, calculates forgiveness timelines, and generates a personalized action plan. Charge $99-199 per analysis (mass market) or $499 for premium with ongoing monitoring. Layer in refinancing affiliate revenue ($200-1,000 per conversion). The SAVE crisis is your launch marketing.

## Key Insight Across All 10

The pattern that separates Tier 1 from Tier 3 is clear:

```
HIGH FIT for solo AI operator:
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  1. Work is primarily paperwork/form-filling          │
│  2. Customers are confused and need guidance           │
│  3. Licensed professionals can be contracted, not      │
│     employed (attorney, CPA, doctor oversight)         │
│  4. Existing providers are expensive and slow          │
│  5. Digital acquisition channels work (social, SEO)    │
│  6. Recurring revenue possible                         │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

LOW FIT for solo AI operator:
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  1. Physical presence required                        │
│  2. Deep personal relationships drive sales           │
│  3. Dominated by well-funded incumbents               │
│  4. One-time purchase (no recurring)                  │
│  5. Emotional product requiring human empathy          │
│  6. High liability for errors                          │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

The businesses that fit the Medvi model are those where AI replaces the *operations layer* (intake, form-filling, coordination, communication) while licensed humans provide the *trust layer* (attorney signature, doctor prescription, CPA sign-off). The solo operator's job is to build the machine that connects confused customers to licensed professionals through an AI-powered workflow.
